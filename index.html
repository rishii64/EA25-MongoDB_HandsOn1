MongoDB HandsOn 1:

  1. Query the collection "employee" and list all the documents
    2. A: command: db.employee.find({}) output: gives us a collection of 13 datas
  2. Query the collection "employee" and list the employees who are having salary more than 30000
    3. A: command: db.employee.find({"salary":{$gt:"30000"}}) output: gives us a collection of 6 datas
  3. Query the collection "employee" and list the employees who are having experience more than 2 years.
    4. A: command: db.employee.find({"overallExp":{$gt:"2"}}) output: shows no data since none of the employees have experience > 2 yrs
  4. Query the collection "employee" and list the employees who are graduated after 2015 and having experience more than 1 year
    5. A: command: db.employee.find({"yearGrad":{$gt:"2015"},"overallExp":{$gt:"1"}}) output: gives us a collection of 4 datas
  5. Query the collection "employee" and update the salary of the employee whose salary is greater than 70000 to 65000.
    6. A: command: db.employee.updateMany({"salary":{$gt:"70000"}},{$set:{"salary":"65000"}}) output: no output since no employess have salary > 70000
  6. Delete all the documents from "employee" where last company is Y
    7. A: command: db.employee.deleteMany({"lastCompany":"Y"}) output: deleted a collection of 6 datas
